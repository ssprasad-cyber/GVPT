About Department

ORIGIN AND GROWTH

The Human Resource Management Department was established in the year 2002 with an intention to promote management education in this region. The Master of Human Resource Management programme is intended to develop managerial skills in different functional areas of management with practical focus on Human Resource Management. The Department has been very fortunate in having the expertise of senior academicians and intellectuals who have been instrumental in shaping the institution to its present status with their vision and teaching. The department is currently having two senior faculty members led by Sri.B.Sivaprasad (submitted his Ph.D work in the area of HRD) and duly supported by a senior most faculty Dr.A.V.Jogarao. The faculty members are multi skilled with academic and industrial experience and having a keen interest towards the research besides the teaching.

ACADEMIC PROGRAMME

The course curriculum consists of 24 papers spread across 4 semesters and the field work programme is an integral part of the course curriculum. Besides this, to evaluate the students’ performance, two mid-term examinations in each subject will be held during the semester (Mid-Exam-I Descriptive; Mid-Exam II is online objective test) and internal viva-voce examination will be conducted in each semester after completion of semester end examination. The intake of students has been increased from 30 to 40 w.e.f 2007-08 academic year. Permanent affiliation has been granted by Andhra University in the academic year 2007-08. MHRM Programme entered the autonomous stream in 2009-10.

Our Mission And Vision

MISSION :

The department of Human Resources will design, develop and deliver efficient and contemporary human resource programs intended to reach out the vision through collaborative efforts of individual and institution and thereby gaining recognition, respect and reputation of stakeholders.

VISION :

Empowering the community of HR Excellence through education and training for enhanced organizational performance.


Core Values

  • Honesty and Discipline first.
  • Respect and Virtues.
  • Model Leadership.
  • Develop trust.
  • Encourage team Work.
  • Promote Educational Quality.
  • Training of students by customization.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

  • PSO1: Students would be able to identify, define and apply the principles of HRM effectively at work place and more importantly, translate them into reality.
  • PSO2: Students would be able to gauge human capital needs in the context of dynamic organisational environment.
  • PSO3: Students would be able to develop appropriate HR strategies to aid in HR planning and decision making processes of an organisation with an ultimate goal of augmenting productivity.
  • PSO4: Able to nurture a yearning for learning HR competencies for organisational growth and brand image.
  • PSO5: Would be able to evaluate human capital management practices in the global context.

HOD's Message

In the context of intensified competition in the corporate and fast changing economic scenario world over the Human Resource Management has assumed greater significance. In spite of economic growth, the common man appears to be more insecure and hence he is in search of quick wealth and fast living. This compelled him to lose focus on Ethics, Values and Human culture. It is time that, HRM practitioners and experts shall have to play new and innovative roles besides strengthening the HRM Processes in the organization. To ensure this phenomenon one need to know and understand the practices and systems of Human Resource Management in organizational environment. To make this possible, a Programme by name M.H.R.M (Master’s in Human Resource Management) has been designed to develop the know-how and skills in different functional areas of management with specific practical focus on Human Resource Management.

Dr. A.V.Joga Rao.
Ph.D
Professor & H.O.D

Value Addition:

Industrial Visits:

As Field work is an integral component of course curriculum, in each semester students will be provided with an opportunity to interact with reputed industries and govt agencies to get practical exposure.

Personality Development Activities:

The Department has been organizing Personality Development sessions on every Friday to give the additional inputs to the students and to make them to suit the present organisational needs. The PD Session is consisting of Case studies, Group Discussions, Presentations, Role Play, Business Games, HR Quiz etc., and emphasis is made on success stories of great entrepreneurs which motivate the student fraternity.
