About the Program

The BA Honours (Sanskrit) is a distinguished three-year undergraduate program dedicated to the study of Sanskrit language, literature, and culture. This course is meticulously designed for students who aspire to delve deep into the classical language of India, exploring its rich heritage and profound philosophical texts.

Students will engage with a comprehensive curriculum that includes classical Sanskrit literature, Vedic texts, Indian philosophy, and linguistics. The program emphasizes both the theoretical and practical aspects of the language, fostering a deep understanding of ancient texts and their contemporary relevance. Special focus is given to areas such as Sanskrit grammar, poetics, and dramaturgy.

Through rigorous academic training and research-oriented projects, students develop critical thinking, analytical skills, and a profound appreciation for Sanskrit's literary and cultural contributions. The program also includes practical components such as manuscriptology and epigraphy, providing hands-on experience in deciphering and interpreting ancient scripts.

Guided by a team of experienced scholars, students benefit from access to extensive library resources, digital archives, and opportunities for interdisciplinary research. Graduates of this program are well-equipped for careers in academia, research, cultural preservation, and translation. They can also pursue higher studies in Sanskrit or related fields, contributing to the preservation and promotion of India's classical heritage.

The BA Honours (Sanskrit) program is an ideal choice for those passionate about exploring the depths of Sanskrit literature and its enduring impact on Indian culture and philosophy.
